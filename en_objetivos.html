Objective

From A. Neem Bangalore, we promote the social integration of the leprosy people and their families, helping them to lead a more worthy life and giving them the possibility to have their own economic resources in a world that keeps advancing each day, as well as the respect, interaction and economic empowerment of the women in the  Indian society.  Doing so, we intend to break with the worldwide taboo about the leprosy illness and fight for its total eradication, as well as for a world of equality between men and women.  The working fields at Neem keep growing focused on gender aspects development, development, income, infrastructures, sanity and health in each and every one of our different projects.

Projects

Our Association’s crucial spots are centered in three suburbs of Bangalore:  (Chikkanayakanahalli, Indirapuram and Shanti Nilayan)

  • Chikkanayakanahalli

  • Located in the outskirts and to the southeast of this great metropolis, it is composed of 44 families in which one or various members suffer from leprosy. During the past few years, there have been great investments in hours and effort, to provide them with better quality of life  through workshops focused in consolidating social-economical and gender matters as well as enhancing their economic independence and settling values that can strengthen the role of  women in the Indian Society. Some of the workshops are: agriculture, jewelry, candles, sexual hygiene, hygiene, gender, social relations, leprosy ilness, HIV, etc. Such workshops are part of the project: "Training project to identify business opportunities and the social inclusion of disabled leprosy women and their families of the suburb of Chikkanayakanahalli, in Bangalore, India.
  • This project is financed jointly by the City Hall of Barcelona and Cambrils. Still, there is lots of daily work being done to assure better living conditions and the improvement of their dwellings and of the community in general.

  • Indirapuram

  • Situated in a suburb on the northern area of Bangalore, it shelters 35 kids, belonging to families with members suffering from leprosy or no economic resources. In this childcare center, we start from the early age to show this new generation some fundamental values, as well as the need to respect  the world that surrounds them and to reinforce in an active way their aptitudes, attitudes and their motor skills. All of this is done in a playful environment, always improving our center with the support of the three staff members that live in the same area. At the end of children’s kindergarten age, we continue to assure all our students private primary education.

  • Shanti Nilayan

  • Situated in the south of Bangalore city, this is a school run by nuns, in which all religions coexist and we contribute to the integral formation of the young, always in an environment of respect, responsibility, teamwork and tolerance.
From A. Neem Bangalore, we intend to prompt the upper, primary, and preschool education, promoting and financing the studies to children and youths that have a leprosy member in their families.
