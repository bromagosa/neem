Mission, vision, goals, values and principles.

The mission, values and principles are a philosophical support in which the Association finds its inspiration to draw the working lines and strategic objectives. They are an indispensable element for scheduling and planning actions and projects that arise from them.

Mission

The Bangalore Neem Association is a non-profit organization with CIF: G-64541246 working in Bangalore, India, since 2005, implementing projects that can improve the living conditions and promote the social inclusion of people affected by the leprosy disease as well as their families living in the "slums" of Chikkanayakanahalli and Indirapuramthrough, through an integral development model, without discrimination on grounds of gender, caste, religion or disease. It works with an organization counterpart named Action Development Trust (DAT) which started in 2009, consisting of sons and daughters of the Chikkanayakanahalli Community of leprosy patients which, knowing the reality of their family and needs for the improvement of the living conditions of the community, organized themselves to canalize the work of A.NEEM Bangalore. A. Neem Bangalore’s aim is to be an accessible organization that can ensure personalized attention to all the actual groups it deals with.

Vision

A. Neem Bangalore is committed to the creation of a Bangalore city where leprosy is eradicated and where its population is fully integrated in the society and able to participate in it, helping the poor and underprivileged ones, without discrimination on grounds of gender, caste, religion or disease.

Objectives

  • Improve the living conditions of the people affected by the leprosy disease and their families, offering new social and employment opportunities to the underprivileged.
  • Improve the infrastructure of the houses where people affected by leprosy and their families live and the basic services in their communities.
  • Promote health and encourage the treatment against leprosy and other opportunistic diseases.
  • Promote and encourage education and literacy in the young and adult population.

Values and principles

  • Respect of the sick , the disabled, the  poor, the marginalized, as well as their families.
  • Respect of the customs, culture and Indian society.
  • Promotion of equality without distinction of gender, caste or religion.
  • Promotion of social network  and Community action.
  • Supply of daily love, understanding and solidarity to the most needy.
